Class Features

Bardic Inspiration

1st, 4th, and 7th level
Action: action

Grant an inspiration die to bolster an ally's roll.

As an action, choose one creature other than yourself within 60 feet that can hear or see you. That creature gains one Bardic Inspiration die (d6 at 1st level, d8 at 4th, d10 at 7th).

Within the next 10 minutes, the creature can roll the die and add the result to one ability check, attack roll, or save. The die is then lost.

Uses per long rest equal to your Willpower modifier (minimum 1). See Font of Inspiration at 3rd level.

Spellcasting

1st-level feature

Arcane spells known; uses Spell Power.

As a conduit for Arcane magic, you cast spells using Spell Power (SP). Spell cost equals spell circle. Your max spell circle by level is shown on the Bard progression table.

You know a fixed list of spells and do not prepare them daily. Learn additional spells at the levels shown on your class progression table.

Spellcasting ability: Willpower (WIL). Spell save DC and spell attack modifier use your proficiency bonus + Willpower (WIL) modifier.

Cantrips & rituals: As shown on your progression table. Rituals do not cost Spell Power when cast using the ritual rules in Magic.

Second action casting: If you cast a spell as your second action (because you did not move this turn), spell attacks are at disadvantage and targets have advantage on saves against that spell.

Expertise

1st and 5th level

Double proficiency on chosen skills or tools.

Choose two skill or tool proficiencies (or one of each). You have advantage on ability checks that use either proficiency.

At 5th level, choose two more proficiencies to gain this benefit.

Bardic Performance

2nd and 5th level

Weave magic into ongoing performances.

As an action, begin a performance (Celebrate Life, Cutting Words, or Clarity of Thought at 5th level). Maintain it by spending an action at the start of each of your turns, up to 1 minute.

  • Celebrate Life. Allies in range can expend a Recovery die at the start of their turn to heal; allies have advantage on death saves.
  • Cutting Words. Reaction while performing: subtract an inspiration die from an enemy's check, attack, or damage roll.
  • Clarity of Thought (5th). Allies in range cannot be charmed and have advantage on saves against fear.

Uses per long rest equal to your proficiency bonus.

Font of Inspiration

3rd-level feature

Regain inspiration on short rest; bolster failed rolls.

You regain all expended uses of Bardic Inspiration when you finish a short or long rest.

When a creature within 60 feet fails a check, attack, or save, you can use your reaction to expend inspiration and add the die to the total, potentially turning failure into success.

Magical Secrets

6th and 9th level

Learn spells from any source.

Choose two spells (or cantrips) from any spell list at 6th level and two more at 9th. Each must be of a circle you can cast. They count as Arcane spells for you.

Grand Performance

8th-level feature

Performance range increases to 60 feet.

Your Bardic Performance now affects creatures within 60 feet that can hear or see you.

Heroic Boon

5th-level feature

Persistent or valiant inspiration options.

Choose one:

  • Persistent Inspiration. If a creature adds your inspiration die and still fails, the die is not lost.
  • Valiant Inspiration. After a successful attack, the creature can expend the die for extra damage; or as a reaction when taking damage, reduce damage by die + your Willpower modifier.

Epic Boon

10th-level feature

Recover inspiration during performance.

Curtain Call. If you start your turn with no Bardic Inspiration uses remaining, you can recover uses equal to your Willpower modifier (minimum 1) as part of the action that maintains your performance. Once per long rest.

Improvement

3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, and 10th level

Ability increases or a utility talent.

At 3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, and 10th level, choose one improvement (ability modifiers cannot exceed +7 with this feature):

  • Increase a single ability modifier by 2.
  • Increase two different ability modifiers by 1 each.
  • Increase one ability modifier by 1 and select a talent from the utility talents list.

Subclass Features

Choose your subclass at 2nd level. Subclass features arrive at 2nd, 4th, 6th, and 8th level.

Lore

A collector of knowledge who weaves magic and talents from every tradition.

Bardic Performance: Ode to Heroes

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Lore

Performance that helps allies and hinders foes on ability checks.

You gain the Ode to Heroes Bardic Performance option. When you perform, choose Fitness, Insight, or Willpower. While your performance lasts, allies in range have advantage on ability checks using that ability and enemies have disadvantage.

Expanded Talent List

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Lore

Choose talents from magic or utility lists.

When you gain a new talent from Improvement, you can select it from either the magic or utility talent lists.

Jack of All Trades

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Lore

Half PB on ability checks that don't already include PB.

Add half your PB (rounded down) to any ability check that doesn't already include your PB.

Swift Ritual

4th-level subclass feature
Subclass: LoreAction: action

Cast a known ritual as an action once per long rest.

You can cast an Arcane ritual you know as an action instead of its listed casting time. You must still provide all other components. Once used, you can't use this again until you finish a long rest.

Magical Rites

6th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Lore

Learn two extra ritual spells from any source.

Learn two ritual spells of your choice from any source list. Each must be of a circle you can cast. They count as Arcane spells for you and don't count against rituals known from your class table.

Peerless Skill

8th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Lore

Spend Bardic Inspiration on ability checks; keep the die on success.

When you make an ability check and have Bardic Inspiration available, you can roll a Bardic Inspiration die and add it to the check. If you succeed, you keep the die; if you fail, the die is expended.

Adapted from BFRD Lore.

Victory

Inspire allies to triumph through martial cadence.

Bardic Performance: Battle March

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Victory

Performance lets allies react-move; medium armor and martial weapon.

You gain Battle March as a Bardic Performance option. When you perform—and as part of each free action spent maintaining it—you can command up to three willing allies who can see or hear you to use their reaction to move up to half their speed without opportunity attacks.

You also gain proficiency with medium armor, shields, and one martial weapon of your choice.

When Improvement grants a new talent, you can choose from the martial or utility talent lists.

Multiattack

4th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Victory

Two attacks when you take the Attack action.

When you take the Attack action on your turn, you can make two attacks instead of one.

Unified Front

6th-level subclass feature
Subclass: VictoryAction: reaction

Reaction: boost your save and allies auto-succeed on area effects.

When you are targeted by an area effect that requires a save, use your reaction to expend a Bardic Inspiration die and add it to your save. On a success, every friendly creature in the area automatically succeeds on their saves as well.

Inspired Strike

8th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Victory

Spend Bardic Inspiration on attacks; keep the die on a hit.

Once per turn when you make a weapon or spell attack and have Bardic Inspiration available, roll a Bardic Inspiration die and add it to the attack roll. If you hit, you keep the die; if you miss, the die is expended.

Mocking Rage

8th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Mockery

Your banter and mockery can enrage creatures. As an

Your banter and mockery can enrage creatures. As an action, choose a number of creatures up to your PB that you can see within 30 feet of you. Each target must make a WIS save against your spell save DC or succumb to a fit of rage for 1 minute. While in such a fit, a target is unable to distinguish friend from foe and must attack the nearest creature on its turn. If no other creature is near enough to move to and attack, the target stalks off in a random direction, seeking a target for its rage. A target can repeat the save at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success.

Adapted from BFRD Mockery.
