Class Features

Explorer

1st-level feature

Unmatched wilderness mobility.

Climbing or swimming speed equal to walking speed. Advantage on tracking checks. Speed not halved in difficult terrain (other penalties still apply).

Mystic Mark

1st level and higher

Mark quarry for extra damage.

When you hit a creature, mark it for 1 minute (extra damage d4 → d6 at 3rd → d8 at 6th → d10 at 9th). Uses = proficiency bonus per long rest.

Martial Action

2nd-level feature

Aim or Quick Strike as second action.

Choose Aim or Quick Strike. Second action when you have not moved (see Martial Action).

Spellcasting

2nd-level feature

Primordial spells known.

As a conduit for Primordial magic, you cast spells using Spell Power (SP). Spell cost equals spell circle. Your max spell circle by level is shown on the Ranger progression table.

You know a fixed list of spells and do not prepare them daily. Learn additional spells at the levels shown on your class progression table.

Spellcasting ability: Insight (INS). Spell save DC and spell attack modifier use your proficiency bonus + Insight (INS) modifier.

Cantrips & rituals: As shown on your progression table. Rituals do not cost Spell Power when cast using the ritual rules in Magic.

Second action casting: If you cast a spell as your second action (because you did not move this turn), spell attacks are at disadvantage and targets have advantage on saves against that spell.

Multiattack

3rd-level feature

Two attacks per Attack action.

Your physical prowess has grown. On your turn, you can make up to two attacks when you take the Attack action.

In YMIAT, each successful weapon attack typically inflicts 1 Wound (2 on a critical hit). Weapon bonuses apply to hit, not to the number of Wounds dealt.

Empowered Mark

4th-level feature

Sense and strike your quarry.

While your mark is within 60 feet, you know its location and it cannot gain advantage from being unseen. No disadvantage from invisibility when attacking it.

Stalker's Step

5th-level feature

Vanish in natural cover.

In dim light, darkness, or natural obscurement, use an action to become invisible until your next turn (ends if you attack or cast). Uses = PB per long rest.

Keensense

7th-level feature

Hearing-based blindsight.

Keensense 10 feet (does not work while deafened).

Strider

8th-level feature

Move without provoking opportunity attacks.

Your movement does not provoke opportunity attacks. Advantage on saves vs grapple, restraint, paralysis, and speed reduction.

Heroic Boon

5th-level feature

Path of the Predator or Sage.

Predator: Mark your quarry when combat begins without needing to hit first; transfer mark when quarry dies. Sage: Learn two extra cantrips and two Primordial rituals.

Epic Boon

10th-level feature

Wisdom guides every blow against your mark.

Add your Insight modifier to the attack or damage roll of each attack against a mystic-marked creature on your turn.

Improvement

3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, and 10th level

Ability increases or martial/utility talent.

At 3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, and 10th level, choose one improvement (ability modifiers cannot exceed +7 with this feature):

  • Increase a single ability modifier by 2.
  • Increase two different ability modifiers by 1 each.
  • Increase one ability modifier by 1 and select a talent from the martial or utility talents list.

Subclass Features

Choose your subclass at 2nd level. Subclass features arrive at 2nd, 4th, 6th, and 8th level.

Hunter

Slayer of supernatural predators threatening the wilds.

Hunter Calling Spells

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Hunter

Always-known hunter spells.

These domain spells are always prepared:

  • Level 2: protection from evil and good
  • Level 5: misty step
  • Level 9: nondetection
  • Level 10: banishment

Killer Instinct

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: HunterAction: free action

Learn target resistances and vulnerabilities.

As a free action, learn a creature's immunities, resistances, and vulnerabilities within 60 feet (blocked by anti-divination). PB uses per long rest.

Relentless Pursuit

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Hunter

Extra damage vs. wounded quarry.

Once per turn when you hit a creature below Max Wounds, deal extra damage equal to your Mystic Mark die (same damage type).

Favored Foe

4th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Hunter

Prepare against a creature type after research.

During a long rest, choose a creature type (Aberration, Beast, Celestial, etc.). For 24 hours, that type has disadvantage attacking you and you have advantage on saves vs. their charm, frighten, or possession.

No Escape

6th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Hunter

Teleport to marked prey that tries to flee.

When a creature marked by Mystic Mark moves, use your reaction to teleport up to 30 feet. If you end within reach, make an opportunity attack as part of the same reaction.

Predator's Shield

8th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Hunter

Resistance to damage from a seen attacker.

When you take damage from a creature you can see, use your reaction to gain resistance to all damage from that creature for 1 minute (including the triggering hit). Once per long rest.

Pack Master

Lead beast companions and coordinated pack tactics.

Beast Spirit

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Pack Master

Ritual-bound beast spirit companion (avian, agile, aquatic, or sturdy).

After a 1-hour ritual (during a rest), bind a beast spirit in a Medium animal form (avian/agile/aquatic/sturdy). It is friendly, shares your initiative (acts after you), and uses your PB in its statistics. Natural weapon: +4 + PB to hit, 1d8 + 4 + PB damage (B/P/S your choice). Multiattack count = half PB (rounded down). At 0 Wounds the body is destroyed; repeat ritual to recreate. See Pack Master stat block in Player's Guide.

Life Link

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Pack MasterAction: reaction

Reaction: take your companion's damage.

When your beast spirit takes damage within 30 feet, use your reaction to take any amount of that damage instead (not reduced by your resistances). PB uses per long rest.

Pack Master Calling Spells

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Pack Master

Always-known pack master calling spells.

These domain spells are always prepared:

  • Level 2: animal friendship
  • Level 5: wildsense
  • Level 9: spirit guardians
  • Level 10: polymorph

Shared Mark

4th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Pack Master

Companion attacks are magical; extra damage vs Mystic Mark.

Your beast spirit's attacks count as magical. While a creature bears your Mystic Mark, the spirit's hits deal extra damage equal to your Mystic Mark die (scales with ranger level).

Soul Bond

6th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Pack MasterAction: reaction

Share spell targeting and delivery with your spirit.

When you are targeted by a spell, your spirit within 30 feet can be targeted too and receives the same benefits. When you cast a non-self spell, your spirit within 100 feet can use its reaction to deliver it using your spellcasting modifier and DC.

Undying Friendship

8th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Pack Master

Spirit sacrifice restores you at 0 Wounds.

At 0 Wounds or instant death while your spirit is active, it can sacrifice itself: its body is destroyed and you return with Wounds equal to those it had. Requires a long rest before you can perform the binding ritual again.
