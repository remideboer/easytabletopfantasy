Class Features

Rage

1st-level feature
Action: free action

Primal fury as a free action; bonus damage and physical resilience.

In battle, you fight with primal ferocity. On your turn, you can rage as a free action.

While raging (and not wearing heavy armor):

  • You have advantage on Fitness ability checks and saves.
  • When you make a melee weapon attack using Fitness, you gain a bonus to damage per the Rage + column on your progression table.
  • You have resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage—attackers have disadvantage on those damage rolls against you.

You cannot cast spells or concentrate on them while raging.

Your rage lasts 1 minute. It ends early if you are knocked unconscious, or if your turn ends and you have not attacked a hostile creature or taken damage from one since the start of your last turn. You can end rage early as a free action.

Uses per long rest: see the Rages column on your progression table.

Unarmored Defense

1st-level feature

High AC without armor when relying on raw toughness.

While you are not wearing armor, your base Armor Class equals 10 + 2 × your Fitness modifier. You can use a shield and still gain this benefit.

Danger Sense

1st-level feature

Advantage on Fitness saves against unseen peril.

You gain an uncanny sense of when things are not as they should be. You have advantage on Fitness saving throws unless you have the Incapacitated condition.

Reckless Attack

1st-level feature

Trade defense for ferocious accuracy.

When you make an attack on your turn, you can attack recklessly. You have advantage on melee weapon attack rolls using Fitness during this turn, but you have disadvantage on your Defense roll until the start of your next turn.

Fast Movement

3rd-level feature

Extra speed and opening repositioning.

Your speed increases by 10 feet while you are not wearing heavy armor. When combat begins and you are not surprised, you can move up to half your speed before any turns are taken. This movement does not use your movement on your turn.

Multiattack

3rd-level feature

Two attacks with the Attack action.

Your physical prowess has grown. On your turn, you can make up to two attacks when you take the Attack action.

In YMIAT, each successful weapon attack typically inflicts 1 Wound (2 on a critical hit). Weapon bonuses apply to hit, not to the number of Wounds dealt.

Feral Instinct

3rd-level feature

First-turn ferocity and surprise immunity when raging.

On the first turn of each combat, you have advantage on melee weapon attack rolls (this stacks with weapon proficiency as normal).

If you are surprised at the start of combat and are not incapacitated, you can act normally on your first turn if you enter a rage before doing anything else on that turn.

When using the initiative variant, you have advantage on initiative rolls.

Brutal Critical

5th, 7th, and 9th level

Expanded critical range and extra critical damage dice.

You score a critical hit on a d20 roll of 19 or 20 with melee weapons and unarmed strikes.

You also roll additional weapon damage dice on melee critical hits:

  • 5th level: 1 extra die
  • 7th level: 2 extra dice
  • 9th level: 3 extra dice

On a critical hit in YMIAT, the attack inflicts 2 Wounds before applying extra dice (exploding 20s still apply).

Heroic Boon

5th-level feature

Choose a powerful rage-enhancing boon.

Choose one heroic boon:

  • Instant Rage. When combat begins and you use Fast Movement's opening move, you can enter a rage without spending a free action if you have a use of Rage remaining.
  • Stubborn Rage. Your rage ends only when its duration expires, you fall unconscious, or you choose to end it.
  • Bloody Rage. While raging, slashing damage from your weapon attacks ignores resistance.
  • Vengeful Rage. When a hostile creature damages you while you are raging, you have stacking advantage on the first attack you make against that creature on your next turn.

Relentless Rage

7th-level feature

Cling to consciousness while raging at Max Wounds.

While raging, if your Wounds reach your Max Wounds, you can make a DC 10 Fitness save. On a success, you do not fall unconscious until your rage ends. You still make death saves as normal.

Each time you use this feature after the first, the DC increases by 5. The DC resets when you finish a short or long rest.

Unyielding Might

9th-level feature

Reliable Fitness and devastating blows against objects.

When you make an ability check or save using Fitness, treat any d20 roll of 9 or lower as a 10.

Your melee weapon attacks deal additional damage equal to your Fitness score (not modifier) against objects and structures.

Epic Boon

10th-level feature

Embody untamed power.

Primal Champion. Your Fitness modifier increases by 2. Your maximum Fitness modifier is now +7.

Improvement

3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, and 10th level

Ability increases or a martial talent.

At 3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, and 10th level, choose one improvement (ability modifiers cannot exceed +7 with this feature):

  • Increase a single ability modifier by 2.
  • Increase two different ability modifiers by 1 each.
  • Increase one ability modifier by 1 and select a talent from the martial talents list.

Subclass Features

Choose your subclass at 2nd level. Subclass features arrive at 2nd, 4th, 6th, and 8th level.

Berserker

Primal bloodlust pushes your rage into a devastating frenzy.

Frenzy

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: BerserkerAction: free action (with Rage)

Optional frenzy with rage; extra melee attack while frenzied.

When you rage, you can choose to enter a frenzy as part of the same free action.

While frenzied, once per turn you can make one extra melee weapon attack as your second action (you must not have moved this turn, per YMIAT second-action rules).

Ruthless Bearing

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Berserker

Intimidation proficiency; double advantage if already proficient.

You gain proficiency in the Intimidation skill. If you are already proficient, you have double advantage on Intimidation checks.

Mindless Rage

4th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Berserker

Ignore exhaustion while raging; immune to charm and frighten; temp HP on crit or kill during frenzy.

While raging, you gain the following benefits:

  • You ignore any levels of exhaustion for the duration of your rage.
  • You can't be charmed or frightened while raging. If you are already charmed or frightened when you enter a rage, the effect is suspended for the duration.
  • During a frenzy, if you score a critical hit or reduce a creature to 0 Wounds, you gain temporary Wounds equal to your PB + your Fitness modifier. These last until expended or until you finish a short or long rest.

Intimidating Presence

6th-level subclass feature
Subclass: BerserkerAction: free action

Bonus damage vs. frightened foes; free action to frighten nearby creatures.

Your weapon attacks deal extra damage equal to your PB (same damage type as the weapon) to frightened creatures.

As a free action, you can attempt to frighten up to PB creatures within 30 feet that can see or hear you. Each target makes an Insight save (DC 8 + PB + Willpower modifier + temporary Wounds from Mindless Rage). On a failure, a creature is frightened of you until the end of your next turn.

On subsequent turns, you can use a free action to extend the effect on each frightened creature until the end of your next turn. The effect ends if the creature ends its turn more than 60 feet away or can no longer see or hear you. On a successful save, you can't use this feature on that creature again for 24 hours.

Retaliation

8th-level subclass feature
Subclass: BerserkerAction: reaction

Reaction: close on attacker and strike back.

When a creature hits or misses you with an attack, you can use your reaction to move up to half your speed toward that creature and make a single melee attack against it. If the creature is Large or larger, you have advantage on the attack roll.

Adapted from BFRD Berserker.

Wild Fury

Animal spirits guide your rage—choose a beast focus at each subclass level.

Animal Focus

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Wild Fury

Choose your first animal focus (2nd level in YMIAT).

At 2nd level, choose an animal focus that shapes how your rage manifests. You choose another focus at 4th, 6th, and 8th level.

Options include: Alligator, Bear, Tiger, Toad, Hawk, Lizard, Turtle, Wolf, Crane, Ram, Shark, and Snake. Each focus grants a thematic benefit while you rage (movement, senses, damage type, or defense). Work with your GM to apply the BFRD Wild Fury focus rules adapted to YMIAT.

Deepening Focus

4th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Wild Fury

Second animal focus (4th level in YMIAT).

Choose a second animal focus. Its benefits stack with your first while you rage.

Primal Synthesis

6th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Wild Fury

Third animal focus (6th level in YMIAT).

Choose a third animal focus. When you rage, you can blend two active focuses for one enhanced effect once per rage.

Apex Predator

8th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Wild Fury

Fourth animal focus (8th level in YMIAT).

Choose a fourth animal focus. While raging, you can move up to half your speed when you enter a rage. Once per combat, if you enter a rage on your first turn, the first successful melee attack you make that turn deals 2 Wounds instead of 1 (unless it is already a critical hit).
