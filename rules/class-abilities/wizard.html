Class Features

Arcane Recovery

1st-level feature

Recover Spell Power on a short rest.

Once per day on a short rest, recover expended spell circles totaling half your wizard level (rounded up) as Spell Power, or per BFRD slot recovery adapted to SP.

Spellcasting

1st-level feature

Prepared Arcane spells from spellbook.

As a conduit for Arcane magic, you cast spells using Spell Power (SP). Spell cost equals spell circle. Your max spell circle by level is shown on the Wizard progression table.

You keep a spellbook. Prepare spells from it equal to your Insight (INS) modifier + your wizard level (minimum one). Add two spells to your spellbook each time you gain a level.

Spellcasting ability: Insight (INS). Spell save DC and spell attack modifier use your proficiency bonus + Insight (INS) modifier.

Cantrips & rituals: As shown on your progression table. Rituals do not cost Spell Power when cast using the ritual rules in Magic.

Second action casting: If you cast a spell as your second action (because you did not move this turn), spell attacks are at disadvantage and targets have advantage on saves against that spell.

Magic Sense

2nd-level feature

Detect magic within 30 feet.

Action: until end of next turn, sense spellcasters, ongoing spell effects, and magic items within 30 feet (blocked by 1 ft stone, 1 in metal, thin lead, 3 ft wood/dirt). Uses = PB + 1 per long rest.

Rote Spell

3rd level and higher

Always-prepared signature spells.

Choose one spell each at 3rd (1st circle), 5th (2nd), 7th (3rd), and 9th (4th) level as always-prepared rote spells. Swap during a long rest.

Superior Recovery

4th-level feature

Swap prepared spells during Arcane Recovery.

When you use Arcane Recovery, swap up to half your proficiency bonus (rounded down) prepared spells from your spellbook.

Spellguard

7th-level feature

Resist hostile magic.

Advantage on saves against spells; resistance to damage from spells and spell attacks.

Spell Mastery

9th-level feature

Cast rote spells without spending slots.

Cast each rote spell once per short rest at lowest circle without spending Spell Power.

Heroic Boon

5th-level feature

Rite of the Ritualist or Source Master.

Ritualist: learn rituals from any magic source. Source Master: add spells from Divine, Primordial, or Wyrd when leveling.

Epic Boon

10th-level feature

Chance to recover spent spell power.

When you cast a spell of 1st circle or higher using Spell Power, roll d10. If the result exceeds the circle spent, recover that Spell Power. Once per short rest.

Improvement

3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, and 10th level

Ability increases or a magic talent.

At 3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, and 10th level, choose one improvement (ability modifiers cannot exceed +7 with this feature):

  • Increase a single ability modifier by 2.
  • Increase two different ability modifiers by 1 each.
  • Increase one ability modifier by 1 and select a talent from the magic talents list.

Subclass Features

Choose your subclass at 2nd level. Subclass features arrive at 2nd, 4th, 6th, and 8th level.

Battle Mage

Arcane offense with martial discipline.

Expanded Talent List

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Battle Mage

Magic or martial talents on Improvement.

When you gain a talent from Improvement, choose from magic or martial talent lists.

Spell Ward

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Battle Mage

AC and physical resistance after casting.

When you cast an Arcane spell of 1st circle+, weave a ward for 1 minute: +PB AC and resistance to B/P/S (ends if unconscious or you end a turn without casting a leveled spell). PB uses per long rest.

Tactical Caster

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Battle Mage

Allies immune to your damaging spells.

When you cast a damaging Arcane spell, choose yourself and allies you can see to be immune to that spell's damage. PB uses per long rest.

Contingency Plan

4th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Battle MageAction: reaction

Redirect missed spell attack to another target.

When a spell attack misses, use your reaction to redirect it to a different target in range with a new attack roll.

Blasting Power

6th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Battle Mage

Empowered spell damage after successful saves.

When a creature succeeds on a save against your Arcane spell but takes no damage, it takes force damage equal to spell circle d6 + Insight modifier.

Counterstrike

8th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Battle Mage

Punish creatures that save against your spells.

When a creature succeeds on a save against your Arcane spell and would suffer no effect, it takes force damage per Blasting Power. Once per turn.

Cantrip Adept

Cantrip mastery and ritual ingenuity.

Arcane Alacrity

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Cantrip AdeptAction: bonus action

Cast action cantrips as bonus actions.

When you cast an Arcane cantrip with casting time 1 action, you can cast it as a bonus action for that casting. PB uses per long rest.

Cantrip Polymath

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Cantrip Adept

Learn cantrips from any magic source.

Learn two cantrips from any spell list; they count as Arcane cantrips and don't count against cantrips known. Any cantrip from lineage or talents also counts as Arcane for you.

Versatile Cantrips

4th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Cantrip Adept

Swap one cantrip after each rest.

When you finish a short or long rest, replace one Arcane cantrip you know with another from your available lists (including Polymath picks).

Potent Cantrip

6th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Cantrip Adept

Add Insight to one cantrip damage roll.

Add your Insight modifier to one damage roll of any Arcane cantrip you cast.

Empowered Cantrips

8th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Cantrip Adept

Maximum damage on cantrips.

Once per turn when you cast a damaging Arcane cantrip, deal maximum damage. PB uses per long rest.

Your ability to collect, decipher, and recall spells is unmatched. When you finish a short or long rest, you can replace one Arcane cantrip that you know with another cantrip from the Arcane source spell list.

Swift Ritual

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Arcanist

As an action, you can cast an Arcane ritual that you have

As an action, you can cast an Arcane ritual that you have scribed within your spellbook, instead of requiring the ritual’s normal casting time. To do so, the ritual can’t be an evocation spell, and it can’t have a casting time greater than 10 minutes. You must still provide all components necessary for the casting of the spell. Once used, you can’t use this feature again until you finish a long rest. 1 CLASS OPTIONS 99

Eidetic Scribe

6th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Arcanist

As a reaction when a creature that you can see and hear

As a reaction when a creature that you can see and hear casts a spell from the Arcane source list, you can attempt to memorize it. Make an INT (Arcana) check with a DC of 10 + the spell’s circle. On a success, you can cast that spell as though you have it prepared until you finish a short or long rest.

Mastered Spell

8th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Arcanist

As part of a long rest, you can select one non‑ritual

As part of a long rest, you can select one non‑ritual spell of 3rd circle or lower that you have scribed within your spellbook and master it. That spell is always prepared for you and doesn’t count against the number of spells you can prepare. You can cast that spell at its lowest circle without expending a spell slot, though you must still supply any spell components.

Adapted from BFRD Arcanist.

Summoner

Conjuration Specialist

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Summoner

You are adept at using magic that creates or summons

You are adept at using magic that creates or summons creatures. You can add any spell from the conjuration school to your spellbook, provided that spell conjures, creates, or summons one or more creatures, such as the find steed and conjure woodland beings spells. These spells count as Arcane spells for you. This feature doesn’t apply to spells from the conjuration school that don’t create or summon actual creatures, such as the guardian of faith and spirit guardians spells. 1 CLASS OPTIONS 101

Unbreakable Focus

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Summoner

You have advantage on CON saves to maintain

You have advantage on CON saves to maintain concentration on spells from the conjuration school.

Master Summoner

4th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Summoner

You can have one additional spell from your Rote Spell

You can have one additional spell from your Rote Spell feature (see Player’s Guide) per spell circle for which you already have a rote spell. These extra rote spells must be spells from the conjuration school that create or summon one or more creatures.

Subclass: Summoner

A friendly creature that isn’t a creature you created or summoned with a spell has advantage on attack rolls against any creature within 5 feet of a creature that you created or summoned with a spell from the conjuration school, provided the creature you created or summoned isn’t incapacitated.

When you cast a spell from the conjuration school that creates or summons a creature, you can create or summon twice as many creatures, unless the spell prevents you from having more than one creature at a time, such as the create familiar spell.
