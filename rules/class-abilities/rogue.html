Class Features

Sneak Attack

1st-level feature

Extra precision damage once per turn.

Once per turn, deal extra Sneak Attack dice when you hit with a finesse or ranged weapon if you have advantage, or if an ally is within 5 feet of the target. Damage per Sneak Attack column.

Expertise

1st and 4th level

Advantage on chosen skill checks.

Choose two proficiencies (four at 4th level) for advantage on related ability checks.

Thieves' Cant

1st-level feature

Secret criminal dialect.

You know Thieves' Cant for hidden messages and recognize criminal signs.

Cunning Action

2nd-level feature
Action: free action

Dash, Disengage, or Hide as a free action.

On your turn, use a free action to Dash, Disengage, or Hide.

Uncanny Dodge

3rd-level feature

Halve damage from one attack.

When an attacker you can see hits you, use your reaction to halve the Wounds from that attack.

Evasion

4th-level feature

Negate area damage on successful saves.

On successful Fitness saves vs area effects, take no damage; half on failure.

Reliable Talent

5th-level feature

Treat low rolls as 10 on proficient checks.

Treat d20 rolls of 9 or lower as 10 on ability checks with skills or tools you are proficient in.

Precise Critical

6th and 8th level

Critical hits on 19–20 with finesse/ranged.

Critical hit on 19–20 with finesse or ranged weapons. Roll 1 extra weapon die on crit at 6th level, 2 extra at 8th.

Keensense

7th-level feature

Hearing-based blindsight 10 feet.

Keensense 10 feet unless deafened.

Elusive

9th-level feature

Attackers rarely have advantage against you.

While not incapacitated, attack rolls cannot have advantage against you.

Heroic Boon

5th-level feature

Escape Artist or Jack of All Trades.

Escape Artist: area saves deal no damage on success. Jack of All Trades: pick talents from any list; gain one immediately.

Epic Boon

10th-level feature

Turn failure into success.

Turn a missed attack into a hit, or treat a failed ability check d20 as 20. Once per short or long rest.

Improvement

3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, and 10th level

Ability increases or a utility talent.

At 3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, and 10th level, choose one improvement (ability modifiers cannot exceed +7 with this feature):

  • Increase a single ability modifier by 2.
  • Increase two different ability modifiers by 1 each.
  • Increase one ability modifier by 1 and select a talent from the utility talents list.

Subclass Features

Choose your subclass at 2nd level. Subclass features arrive at 2nd, 4th, 6th, and 8th level.

Enforcer

Intimidation, control, and ruthless precision.

Ambush

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Enforcer

First-round advantage; auto-crit vs surprised foes.

During the first round of combat, you have advantage on the first attack roll you make against each creature in the combat. Attacks against surprised creatures are automatic critical hits.

Cold-Blooded

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Enforcer

Bonus attack when you drop a foe.

Once per turn when you reduce a creature to 0 Wounds with a weapon attack, immediately make one additional weapon attack against a different creature within range (no Sneak Attack on this bonus attack).

Expanded Talent List

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Enforcer

Martial or utility talents on Improvement.

When you gain a talent from Improvement, choose from the martial or utility talent lists.

Brawler

4th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Enforcer

Sneak Attack in melee without ally adjacency; unarmed eligible.

Sneak Attack works against a target within 5 feet even if no other creatures are adjacent—you don't need advantage but can't have disadvantage. Sneak Attack applies to unarmed strikes.

Ready to Rumble

6th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Enforcer

Can't be surprised; first-turn edge when combat begins.

While not incapacitated, you can't be surprised. When combat begins and you aren't surprised, you have advantage on the first attack roll you make on your first turn.

Kill Shot

8th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Enforcer

Reroll Sneak Attack dice; Sneak Attack on opportunity attacks.

When you roll Sneak Attack damage, reroll any dice (must use new results). You can apply Sneak Attack to an eligible opportunity attack if you haven't used Sneak Attack this round.

Thief

Climb, appraise, and strike before foes react.

Fast Hands

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Thief

Cunning Action can sleight of hand, use object, or pick locks.

Your Cunning Action free action can also: Sleight of Hand; Use an Object; or use thieves' tools to disarm a trap or open a lock.

Second-Story Work

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Thief

Climbing speed and improved jumps.

Climbing speed equals walking speed (+10 ft if you already had climbing speed). Long jump uses walking speed with 10 ft run-up; standing long jump half speed. May use Acrobatics instead of Athletics for jump checks.

Appraising Eye

4th-level subclass feature
Subclass: ThiefAction: action

Identify value and magic of objects.

Action to inspect an object within 10 feet: learn if nonmagical (and value) or magical (1 minute study reveals type, rarity, curse, attunement; can ignore class requirements after study).

Trap Specialist

6th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Thief

Reaction disarm traps including magical ones.

When you or an ally within 5 feet triggers a trap, reaction to disarm it. Magical traps: ability check using Fitness modifier vs. trap DC.

Thief's Reflexes

8th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Thief

Two turns in the first round of combat.

When combat begins and you are not surprised, on your first turn you may take a second action as if you had moved 0 feet, without the usual penalties for a second action. You can't use this feature if you are surprised.

Improved Traps

6th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Trapsmith

You can now create a trap as an action and set it as a bonus

You can now create a trap as an action and set it as a bonus action. When you set a trap you created, it can now be in any unoccupied space you can see within 30 feet of you, and the distance at which you can remotely trigger a trap with the Rework Trap feature increases to 60 feet.

When you fail to disarm a trap that automatically triggers when a disarm attempt fails, the trap doesn’t trigger, and you can try to disarm the trap again. If you fail a second time, the trap triggers.
