Class Features

Last Stand

1st-level feature
Action: reaction

Emergency healing when badly wounded.

When damage would bring your Wounds above half your Max Wounds, you can use your reaction to spend hit dice (up to your proficiency bonus). Roll them and heal Wounds equal to the total + your Fitness modifier.

Martial Action

1st-level feature

Tactical second action when you hold your ground.

Choose one martial action: Aim, Guard, Quick Strike, or Wind Up (see BFRD Fighter).

In YMIAT, use this as your second action when you have not moved this turn. If the second action is an attack, that attack is at disadvantage.

Action Surge

2nd, 7th, and 10th level

Gain an additional action on your turn.

On your turn, gain another action in addition to your normal action. Once per short rest at 2nd level; twice at 7th; three times at 10th (still only once per turn).

Multiattack

3rd, 6th, and 9th level

Multiple attacks per Attack action.

Make two attacks with the Attack action at 3rd level, three at 6th, and four at 9th.

In YMIAT, each successful weapon attack typically inflicts 1 Wound (2 on a critical hit).

Heroic Boon

5th-level feature

Defiant saves or unstoppable recovery.

Choose one:

  • Defiant. When you fail a save, succeed instead. Once per long rest (twice at 7th, three times at 9th).
  • Unstoppable. At the start of your turn, end one of: blinded, charmed, frightened, incapacitated, paralyzed, or stunned. Uses equal to proficiency bonus per long rest.

Epic Boon

10th-level feature

Devastating blows that ignore defenses.

Once per turn when you hit with a weapon attack, deal extra damage equal to your Fitness or Insight score (your choice). The damage ignores resistance and immunity and cannot be reduced.

Improvement

3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, and 10th level

Ability increases or a martial talent.

At 3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, and 10th level, choose one improvement (ability modifiers cannot exceed +7 with this feature):

  • Increase a single ability modifier by 2.
  • Increase two different ability modifiers by 1 each.
  • Increase one ability modifier by 1 and select a talent from the martial talents list.

Subclass Features

Choose your subclass at 2nd level. Subclass features arrive at 2nd, 4th, 6th, and 8th level.

Spell Blade

Blend arcane magic with weapon mastery.

Arcane Spellcasting

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Spell Blade

Partial Arcane spellcasting (abjuration/evocation focus).

You cast Arcane spells using Insight as your spellcasting ability (save DC 8 + PB + Insight; attack + PB + Insight).

At 2nd level: learn 2 cantrips and 3 1st-level abjuration or evocation spells (one may be from any school). You have 2 1st-level slots, regained on a long rest. Learn additional cantrips and spells at 4th, 6th, and 8th level per the Spell Blade table in the Player's Guide (adapted to YMIAT spell levels).

When Improvement grants a talent, choose from magic or martial lists.

Enchant Weapon

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Spell BladeAction: bonus action

Imbue a weapon (+1, scaling to +3); bonus action summon.

After 1 hour of focus on a weapon (during a rest), imbue it as a magic weapon: bonus action to summon to your hand if on the same plane; +1 to attack and damage (stacks with existing magic bonuses). Bonus becomes +2 at 6th level and +3 at 8th. Only one enchanted weapon at a time.

Spell Multiattack

4th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Spell Blade

Replace one Multiattack swing with a cantrip.

When you use Multiattack, you can replace one attack with casting a cantrip you know (casting time 1 action). Only one cantrip per Attack action.

Follow Through

6th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Spell Blade

Weapon attack after casting a spell.

When you take the Cast a Spell action, you can make one weapon attack as part of that same action if a target is in range.

Charged Strike

8th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Spell Blade

Add Insight to melee damage; weapon damage counts as magical.

Add your Insight modifier (minimum +1) to melee weapon damage in addition to Fitness or Dexterity. All weapon damage you deal is magical.

Weapon Master

Mastery of chosen weapons and tactical stunts.

Mastery

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Weapon Master

Master three weapon types; reroll damage or weapon-option damage.

Choose three simple or martial weapon types you have proficiency with. Once per turn when you roll damage with a mastered weapon, reroll the weapon damage dice and use either result. Once per turn with a mastered weapon option, you can roll damage even if the option normally wouldn't.

Stunts

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Weapon Master

Tactical maneuvers with weapon-specific requirements.

Stunt save DC = 8 + PB + Fitness modifier. Uses per short or long rest = PB + 1. One stunt per turn.

  • Arcing Strike (heavy melee): splash half damage to a second target in reach.
  • Cheap Shot (melee): extra unarmed strike on hit during Attack action.
  • Make It Count: single attack at +10; extra damage equal to fighter level.
  • Parry (melee, reaction): reduce damage by 1d10 + PB.
  • Redirect (reaction): reroll missed attack against adjacent target.
  • Riposte (melee, reaction): attack when enemy misses you.
  • Straight Through (ranged): splash half damage to adjacent target.
  • Tactical Retreat (melee): move half speed after hit without opportunity attacks.

Deadly Flourish

4th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Weapon Master

19–20 crits with mastered weapons; reaction attacks on crit.

Attacks with mastered weapons crit on 19–20. On a crit, use your reaction to either: make a ranged attack against up to PB creatures within 15 feet of the target, or make a separate melee attack against each creature within 5 feet of you.

Advanced Stunts

6th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Weapon Master

Powerful stunts available whenever you could use a stunt.

  • Assassin's Ambush: double damage dice from hidden.
  • Bulwark (shield, reaction): reduce spell damage by AC.
  • Felling Sweep (heavy melee, action): one attack roll vs. all in reach.
  • Preemptive Strike (melee, reaction): attack when foe enters reach.
  • Rapid Release (ranged/thrown): bonus attack after Attack action.
  • Wrestler's Clutch (melee): grapple on hit; stunts vs. grappled foe don't cost uses.

Grand Finale

8th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Weapon Master

Regain stunt uses when combat begins with none left.

When combat begins with no Stunt uses remaining, regain 3 uses. Once per long rest.

Adapted from BFRD Weapon Master.

Mighty Fists

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Scrapper

Your unarmed attacks become more powerful. When

Your unarmed attacks become more powerful. When you make an unarmed strike, the damage increases from 1 + your STR modifier to 1d4 + your STR modifier. This further increases to 1d6 at 7th level, 1d8 at 11th level, and 1d10 at 15th level.

Grappling Maneuvers

4th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Scrapper

As a bonus action while grappling a creature, you can

As a free action while grappling a creature, you can perform one of the following grappling maneuvers. You must make a STR (Athletics) check contested by the grappled creature’s STR (Athletics) or DEX (Acrobatics) check (target’s choice). If you succeed, the target is affected by the maneuver until the grapple ends or until you use a different grappling maneuver on it: • Chokehold. You choke a breathing or speaking creature, and it can’t speak or cast spells with verbal components. If the target remains in a chokehold for two consecutive turns, it begins suffocating and continues suffocating until the grapple or this maneuver ends. • Makeshift Shield. You pull the creature between you and danger. You have half cover if the target is your size or smaller and three-quarters cover if it is larger than you. • Pin. You pin, hold, or otherwise immobilize the creature. The target is restrained.

Knockout Punch

8th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Scrapper

When you hit a creature with two unarmed strikes in

When you hit a creature with two unarmed strikes in the same turn (and it has less than half of its Wound maximum) or when you score a critical hit with an unarmed strike, the target must succeed on a CON save (DC 8 + your PB + your STR modifier) or be stunned for 1 minute. The target can repeat the save at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success.

Adapted from BFRD Scrapper.

Blade Dance

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Twinblade

As a bonus action, you can begin a blade dance as long

As a free action, you can begin a blade dance as long as you are neither grappled or restrained nor your speed is otherwise reduced to 0. While engaged in a blade dance, you gain the following benefits if you are wielding a melee weapon in each hand and aren’t wearing medium or heavy armor: • You have advantage on DEX saves and DEX ability checks made to avoid being grappled or restrained or having your movement speed reduced. If you become grappled or restrained, you have advantage on checks made to escape or end the effect as long as your blade dance is active. • Your movement doesn’t provoke opportunity attacks. • Your speed increases by 10 feet.

Deadly Grace

2nd-level subclass feature
Subclass: Twinblade

You gain proficiency in either the Acrobatics or Performance

You gain proficiency in either the Acrobatics or Performance skill. If you are already proficient in one or both of these skills, double your PB for checks made with that skill.

Double Edged

4th-level subclass feature
Subclass: Twinblade

When you take the Attack action on your turn and then use a free action to make one or more additional attacks (such as for two-weapon fighting and the Quick Strike martial action), you can add your DEX modifier to the damage dealt by the bonus attacks. If you have at least one use remaining of your Blade Dance feature, you can activate it as part of the same free action you used to make these attacks.
