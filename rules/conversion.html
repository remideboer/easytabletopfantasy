Converting from D&D 5e to You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT)

On this page

This guide explains how to convert characters, rules, and mechanics from Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition (D&D 5e) to You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT). Use this guide when you have existing D&D 5e characters or content that you want to adapt for You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT).

Converting Ability Scores

When you convert an existing fifth edition character, use this procedure to derive the three You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) abilities. This process is only used during conversion; after conversion, you record modifiers only.

  1. Calculate composite scores.
    • Fitness: Add Strength, Dexterity, and Constitution, divide the total by 3, and round down.
    • Insight: Add Intelligence and Wisdom, divide the total by 2, and round down.
    • Willpower: Add Wisdom and Charisma, divide the total by 2, and round down.
  2. Determine modifiers. For each composite score, determine the modifier using the standard fifth edition formula:
Ability Modifier modifier = ⌊ (score − 10) ÷ 2 ⌋

Record only the resulting three modifiers on the You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) character sheet. The intermediate composite scores are not used in play.

Example: Converting Ability Scores

Given the following fifth edition scores: Strength 14, Dexterity 10, Constitution 13, Intelligence 11, Wisdom 16, Charisma 12.

  • Fitness = (14 + 10 + 13) / 3 = 37 / 3 = 12 (round down) → modifier +1
  • Insight = (11 + 16) / 2 = 27 / 2 = 13 (round down) → modifier +1
  • Willpower = (16 + 12) / 2 = 28 / 2 = 14 → modifier +2

The You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) modifiers are: Fitness +1, Insight +1, Willpower +2.

Converting Levels

You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) characters advance from level 1 to level 10. Every two fifth edition levels combine into one You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) level:

  • Levels 1–2 → You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) level 1
  • Levels 3–4 → You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) level 2
  • Levels 5–6 → You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) level 3
  • Levels 7–8 → You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) level 4
  • Levels 9–10 → You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) level 5
  • Levels 11–12 → You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) level 6
  • Levels 13–14 → You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) level 7
  • Levels 15–16 → You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) level 8
  • Levels 17–18 → You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) level 9
  • Levels 19–20 → You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) level 10

This structure ensures that each new level provides a noticeable increase in power and that class progression, including spellcasting progression, remains coherent and easy to track.

Converting Proficiency

When converting from D&D 5e or Tales of the Valiant, proficiency bonuses added to ability checks, attack rolls, or Defense are replaced by advantage on the relevant rolls. Proficiency is typically granted by your class, background, or talents.

Your proficiency level in You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) equals your You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) level (which is your D&D 5e level divided by 2, rounded up).

If the source material instead calls for the flat proficiency bonus number itself—added to damage, DCs, uses per rest, or similar—convert it to YMIAT's Proficiency Bonus (PB) instead: PB = level ÷ 2, rounded up. See Proficiency and Advantage for details.

When converting from fifth edition, record only the skills the character actually possesses. This approach keeps character sheets compact and supports clear, consistent roleplaying.

Converting Max Wounds

You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) uses a compressed Wounds scale. Most creatures have roughly one-tenth of their fifth edition hit points (rounded up) as Max Wounds. Creatures start at 0 Wounds (WD).

For player characters, use the You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) Max Wounds formula:

Max Wounds Max Wounds = class Max Wounds base + Fitness modifier + ( level − 1 )

Harm adds Wounds; healing removes Wounds. A successful weapon attack typically inflicts 1 Wound.

Converting Weapons

Weapon damage dice from fifth edition convert to fixed bonuses to hit in You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT):

  • Unarmed: +0
  • d4: +1
  • d6: +2
  • d8: +3
  • d10: +4
  • d12: +5
  • 2d6: +6

Weapon attack rolls use the following formula:

Attack Roll d20 + weapon bonus + ability modifier + (advantage if proficient)

Example: Weapon Attack Roll

A fighter with Fitness +2 and a shortsword (attack bonus +2) who is proficient with martial weapons makes an attack roll with advantage (roll 2d20, take highest) + 2 (weapon) + 2 (Fitness) = highest of 2d20 + 4.

On a hit, the attack inflicts 1 Wound. You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) assumes that better weapons improve the chance to hit, while spells generally increase the number of Wounds inflicted and/or apply additional effects.

Converting Wounds (Spells and Effects)

Harm and healing expressed in dice are converted into Wounds for You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT). To convert dice-based values from fifth edition:

  1. Take the maximum total of the dice expression.
  2. Divide by 6.
  3. Round the result to the nearest whole number.

Examples:

  • 3d4 → maximum 12 → 12 / 6 = 2 Wounds
  • 1d8 → maximum 8 → 8 / 6 ≈ 1 Wound
  • 2d8 → maximum 16 → 16 / 6 ≈ 3 Wounds

For mixed expressions, convert dice and flat bonuses separately, then add the results.

Example: 2d4 + 5

  • Dice: maximum 8 → 8 / 6 ≈ 1 Wound
  • Static bonus: 5 / 6 ≈ 1 Wound
  • Total: 2 Wounds.

Converting Armor Class

You-Meet-In-A-Tavern (YMIAT) uses a Defense system instead of Armor Class. When converting from D&D 5e:

  • Defense bonus = AC - 10
  • For unarmored characters: Defense = Fitness modifier (instead of 10 + DEX modifier)

As an option, a group can instead retain the standard fifth edition Armor Class rules for resolving attacks against player characters.

Converting Hit Dice and Recovery

During a short rest, a character gains a pool of Recovery Points equal to the character's level. The player can spend Recovery Points to heal Wounds and/or regain Spell Power in any combination.

A character's maximum Recovery Points equal the character's level. During a long rest, a character regains half of their total Recovery Points (rounded up), similar to the way hit dice are recovered in the core fifth edition rules.
